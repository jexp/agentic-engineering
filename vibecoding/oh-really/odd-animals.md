## Animals

### 1. The Blobfish (Context Rot)
**Animal**: A melting blobfish slumped over a keyboard, its face sliding off with each new token  
**Concept**: Context rot — the agent's quality degrades as the context window fills. The blobfish is only ugly out of water; your agent only degrades under pressure.  
**Title**: *"Context Rot: When the Session Runs Long"*  
**Midjourney prompt**: `an extremely dejected blobfish sitting at a desk covered in sticky notes and coffee cups, its face literally melting and sliding off the page, surrounded by floating JSON tokens, line art engraving style, O'Reilly book cover`

---

### 2. The Axolotl (Ralphing)
**Animal**: A cheerful axolotl pressing a large red button labeled "RALPH," feathery gills spread wide, surrounded by a burst of fresh confetti — it just wiped its context and it is delighted  
**Concept**: Ralphing — context reset as a feature, not a failure. Axolotls regenerate limbs; agents regenerate clarity.  
**Title**: *"Ralph: The Context Reset That Saves You"*  
**Midjourney prompt**: `a cheerful axolotl with dramatic feathery gills pressing a big red button labeled RALPH, surrounded by confetti and swirling forgotten memories, huge smile, line art engraving style, O'Reilly book cover`

---

### 3. The Capybara (Navigator/Driver)
**Animal**: A calm capybara pointing at a whiteboard with one hand while a tiny robot frantically types on a keyboard below — the capybara is not stressed; it has a plan  
**Concept**: Navigator/driver — the human holds the map, the agent executes. Capybaras are famously calm next to any kind of chaos.  
**Title**: *"The Navigator: You Point, It Drives"*  
**Midjourney prompt**: `a serene capybara standing at a whiteboard, pointing at a diagram with a pointer stick, while a small eager robot frantically types on a keyboard at its feet, line art engraving style, O'Reilly book cover`

---

### 4. The Tardigrade (Reliability Under Pressure)
**Animal**: A microscopic water bear wearing a tiny hardhat, holding an even tinier CI badge labeled "passing", walking calmly through a meteor strike, a volcano, and a code review  
**Concept**: Tardigrades survive anything. Your tests and constraints should too. "Prompts are suggestions. CI is a gate."  
**Title**: *"The Indestructible Test: CI Gates and Surviving Everything"*  
**Midjourney prompt**: `a microscopic tardigrade (water bear) wearing a tiny construction helmet, holding a badge that says "CI: passing", walking calmly through explosions, meteor showers, and a massive pull request diff, line art engraving style, O'Reilly book cover`

---

### 5. The Mantis Shrimp (Specs)
**Animal**: A mantis shrimp in a lab coat, one claw holding a precisely formatted specification document, the other claw smashing a keyboard with a loud "SPEC FIRST"  
**Concept**: The mantis shrimp sees 16 color channels — a perfect spec sees every edge case before the code is written. Punch first, ask questions never.  
**Title**: *"Spec First: Seeing What You're Building Before You Build It"*  
**Midjourney prompt**: `a mantis shrimp in a scientist's lab coat, one claw delicately holding a detailed specification document, the other claw dramatically smashing a keyboard, 16 glowing color channels radiating from its compound eyes, line art engraving style, O'Reilly book cover`

---

### 6. The Pangolin (Defensive Engineering)
**Animal**: A curled-up pangolin whose overlapping scales are labeled: "tests," "lint," "typecheck," "secrets scan," "review gate" — each scale a layer of the harness  
**Concept**: Constraints as armor. A pangolin's overlapping scales are defense-in-depth. So is your CI pipeline.  
**Title**: *"The Harness: Defense in Layers"*  
**Midjourney prompt**: `a curled-up pangolin whose overlapping scales are each labeled with words: TESTS, LINT, TYPECHECK, SECRET SCAN, REVIEW GATE, the pangolin looks very smug and secure, line art engraving style, O'Reilly book cover`

---

### 7. The Narwhal (Reversibility)
**Animal**: A narwhal whose tusk has been fully replaced three times — the tusk stubs are labeled "Zig," "Rust," "TypeScript" in sequence — the narwhal doesn't mind, it's used to this  
**Concept**: Agent-enabled reversibility — technology lock-in dissolves when rewrites are cheap. Mitchell Hashimoto: "Programming languages used to be LOCK IN."  
**Title**: *"Lock-In is Over: Technology Choices as Experiments"*  
**Midjourney prompt**: `a narwhal underwater whose horn has been replaced multiple times, with three stubs labeled ZIG, RUST, TYPESCRIPT, the narwhal looking entirely unbothered and slightly philosophical, line art engraving style, O'Reilly book cover`

---

### 8. The Quokka (Normalization of Deviance)
**Animal**: A grinning quokka taking a selfie while a server rack burns behind it; the selfie caption reads "it worked last time!"  
**Concept**: Normalization of deviance — every successful shortcut raises the threshold for the next one. The quokka is the most photographed animal because of its smile; it has no survival instinct.  
**Title**: *"It Worked Last Time: The Normalization of Deviance"*  
**Midjourney prompt**: `a very happy quokka taking a selfie on a smartphone, smiling broadly, while a server rack burns dramatically in the background, caption on the phone screen reads 'it worked last time!', line art engraving style, O'Reilly book cover`

## Usage notes

- Print 8-up on a sheet; hand them out during the ralphing slide
- Use as chapter dividers in the workshop deck (one per act)
- The Quokka and Blobfish are the strongest standalone memes — lead with those on social
- Generate at 1024×1024, then resize to O'Reilly 6×9 book dimensions (648×882px at 108dpi)
- For keynote: the Axolotl (Ralph) works as a slide background with the word "RALPH"
